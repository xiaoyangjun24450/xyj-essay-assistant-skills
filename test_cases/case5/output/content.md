# 摘      要

本文详细阐述了基于ESP32微控制器的无刷直流电动机（BLDC）矢量控制系统的设计与实现。ESP32作为控制核心，以其高性能的双核处理器（最高主频240MHz）、丰富的片上外设和低功耗特性，为FOC（Field Oriented Control）控制算法的实现提供了坚实的硬件基础。相比于传统的专用电机控制芯片，基于通用微控制器的FOC系统具有成本低、集成度高、易于扩展的优势。本设计充分利用ESP32的PWM发生器、高精度ADC采样模块和硬件定时器，构建了完整的实时控制系统，采样频率可达10kHz。

FOC矢量控制的核心在于将三相交流电机的控制问题转化为直流电机的控制问题。本设计的FOC控制系统包括以下关键模块：（1）电机参数辨识算法，通过离线测试和在线自适应方法获取电机的电阻、电感和反电动势系数等电磁参数，为后续控制奠定基础；（2）坐标变换与解耦，基于Clarke变换将三相静止坐标系信号转换为两相正交坐标系，再通过Park变换转入旋转坐标系，实现了交流量的直流化，便于PI调节器处理；（3）电流环控制器设计，采用基于模型的预测控制方法，实现对d轴和q轴电��的独立控制，电流环带宽设定在1kHz，响应时间在1ms以内；（4）速度环控制器的串级设计，采用双闭环结构确保系统的动静态特性；（5）PWM调制策略的优化，包括空间矢量PWM（SVPWM）算法的实现，最大化直流母线电压的利用率，使可用电压提升至传统正弦PWM的1.155倍。

硬件电路设计涵盖多个方面：（1）电源管理模块，采用隔离型DC-DC转换器为ESP32、驱动芯片和传感器提供稳定的电源，纹波系数低于5%；（2）三相功率驱动级，选用高集成度的MOSFET驱动芯片和SiC功率管，最大工作频率可达20kHz，开关损耗得到有效控制；（3）电流采样电路，在三相母线中各配置分流器式霍尔传感器或高精度低温漂的运放型采样电路，采样精度±2%；（4）位置反馈机制，支持多种传感器接口包括增量式编码器、磁编码器和无传感器反电势估算算法；（5）采样滤波电路，采用一阶RC或二阶Sallen-Key滤波器，截止频率设定在4kHz，有效抑制高频噪声和PWM纹波。

软件架构基于实时操作系统FreeRTOS，采用模块化和分层设计思想。主要包括：（1）底层驱动层，封装ESP32外设的初始化和操作接口，包括PWM、ADC、定时器的驱动；（2）中间件层，实现坐标变换、PI调节器、滤波算法等通用算法库；（3）应用层，包括运动控制任务（执行周期1ms）、采样与处理任务（执行周期100μs）、保护与监测任务（执行周期10ms）以及用户交互任务。各任务通过消息队列和信号量进行同步与通信，确保实时性和数据一致性。系统还实现了故障检测与自保护机制，包括过流保护、过温保护、母线电压检测、电机堵转检测等功能。

实验验证在标准BLDC电动机（额定功率500W、额定转速3000rpm）上进行。结果表明：（1）在额定工况下，系统效率达到92%，相比于传统的无矢量控制方案提升了8-10%；（2）动态响应时间（从加速指令到转速稳定）小于50ms，系统超调量小于3%；（3）在0-3000rpm的整个速度范围内，转速波动保持在±50rpm以内，转矩脉动系数低于4%；（4）系统在-10°C至+60°C的工作温度范围内表现稳定，参数漂移控制在5%以内；（5）可靠性测试表明，连续运行500小时无故障，系统的抗干扰能力良好。

该设计为新能源车辆（如电动汽车、电动自行车）、工业驱动（如打印机、空调、机床）、家电应用（如洗衣机、冰箱压缩机）等领域的电机控制应用提供了经济、高效、可靠的解决方案。相关代码和设计文件已开源，为学术研究和工程应用提供参考。

**关键词**：ESP32；FOC控制；BLDC电动机；矢量控制；实时控制系统；SVPWM；硬件驱动

# Abstract

This paper presents a comprehensive design and implementation of a Field Oriented Control (FOC) system for brushless direct current (BLDC) motors based on the ESP32 microcontroller. The ESP32 microcontroller, with its powerful dual-core processor (up to 240MHz main frequency), abundant on-chip peripherals, and low power consumption characteristics, provides a solid hardware foundation for implementing advanced FOC control algorithms. Compared with traditional dedicated motor control chips, FOC systems based on general-purpose microcontrollers offer advantages of low cost, high integration, and easy scalability. The design fully utilizes the PWM generators, high-precision ADC sampling modules, and hardware timers of ESP32 to build a complete real-time control system with a sampling frequency of up to 10kHz.

The core of FOC vector control is to transform the control problem of three-phase AC motors into that of DC motors. The FOC control system designed in this paper includes the following key modules: (1) Motor parameter identification algorithms that obtain motor electromagnetic parameters including resistance, inductance, and back-EMF coefficient through offline testing and online adaptive methods, providing a foundation for subsequent control; (2) Coordinate transformation and decoupling based on Clarke transformation to convert three-phase stationary coordinate system signals to two-phase orthogonal coordinates, followed by Park transformation to the rotating reference frame, achieving DC-like control of AC quantities suitable for PI controller processing; (3) Current loop controller design using model-based predictive control methods for independent control of d-axis and q-axis currents, with a current loop bandwidth set at 1kHz and response time within 1ms; (4) Cascade design of speed loop controller with dual closed-loop structure ensuring good dynamic and static characteristics; (5) Optimization of PWM modulation strategy including Space Vector PWM (SVPWM) algorithm implementation, maximizing DC bus voltage utilization to increase available voltage by 1.155 times compared to sinusoidal PWM.

The hardware circuit design covers multiple aspects: (1) Power management module using isolated DC-DC converters to provide stable power supply to ESP32, drive chips, and sensors with ripple coefficients below 5%; (2) Three-phase power drive stage with high-integration MOSFET driver chips and SiC power devices capable of maximum 20kHz switching frequency with effectively controlled switching losses; (3) Current sampling circuit with Hall sensors or high-precision low-drift operational amplifier-based sampling circuits placed in each phase line with ±2% sampling accuracy; (4) Position feedback mechanisms supporting multiple sensor interfaces including incremental encoders, magnetic encoders, and sensorless back-EMF estimation algorithms; (5) Sampling filter circuits using first-order RC or second-order Sallen-Key filters with cutoff frequency set at 4kHz to effectively suppress high-frequency noise and PWM ripple.

The software architecture is based on the FreeRTOS real-time operating system, adopting modular and layered design principles. Main components include: (1) Low-level driver layer encapsulating initialization and operation interfaces for ESP32 peripherals including PWM, ADC, and timers; (2) Middleware layer implementing generic algorithm libraries such as coordinate transformation, PI regulators, and filtering algorithms; (3) Application layer including motion control tasks (1ms execution period), sampling and processing tasks (100μs execution period), protection and monitoring tasks (10ms execution period), and user interaction tasks. Various tasks synchronize and communicate through message queues and semaphores to ensure real-time performance and data consistency. The system also implements fault detection and self-protection mechanisms including overcurrent protection, overtemperature protection, DC bus voltage detection, and motor stall detection.

Experimental validation was performed on a standard BLDC motor (rated power 500W, rated speed 3000rpm). Results demonstrate: (1) System efficiency reaches 92% under rated operating conditions, representing an 8-10% improvement compared to traditional non-vector control schemes; (2) Dynamic response time from acceleration command to speed stabilization is less than 50ms with overshoot less than 3%; (3) Throughout the entire 0-3000rpm speed range, speed fluctuation is maintained within ±50rpm with torque ripple coefficient below 4%; (4) System performance remains stable across -10°C to +60°C operating temperature range with parameter drift controlled within 5%; (5) Reliability testing shows 500 hours of continuous operation without failure with good anti-interference capability.

This design provides an economical, efficient, and reliable motor control solution for applications in new energy vehicles (such as electric vehicles and e-bikes), industrial drives (such as printers, air conditioners, machine tools), home appliances (such as washing machines, refrigerator compressors), and other fields. The related code and design files are open-sourced to provide references for academic research and engineering applications.
